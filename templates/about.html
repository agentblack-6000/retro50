The "Retrospective" Revision

What is a Retrospective Revision Timetable?

A retrospective revision timetable, in constrast to a regular revision timetable, "plans" to revise weak topics, rather than to revise a topic according to a schedule.
Rather than starting out with a fixed schedule, a retrospective revision timetable allows more flexibility, by starting out with a list of subjects rather than a set of dates. Each time you wish to study/revise, pick a topic which you're struggling with as per the timetable, revise it, and log your proficiency. This will help you identify weak points in your exam/test preparations, until you are proficient with each topic.
The process is as follows-

  1. Study session
  2. Log revision and rate proficiency
  3. Break
  4. During the next study session, look at the table, and decide what you want to revise
  5. Study session
  6. Log revision and rate proficiency
  7. Break
  8. Repeat!
